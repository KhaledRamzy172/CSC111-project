Al Azhar Park Guide

Your ultimate guide to exploring Azhar Park

About the Park

Azhar Park is a serene urban oasis located in the heart of the city, offering a perfect blend of nature and recreation. Spanning over 50 acres, it features lush green lawns, walking trails, and picnic areas. Established in 1995, the park is named after a local philanthropist and serves as a community hub for relaxation, exercise, and family outings. Whether you're looking to jog, birdwatch, or simply unwind, Azhar Park has something for everyone.

History of the Park

The park was created by the Historic Cities Support Programme of the Aga Khan Trust for Culture, an entity of the Aga Khan Development Network. The park was developed at a cost in excess of USD $30 million, its funding a grant to Cairo from Aga Khan IV, a direct descendant of Prophet Muhammad through his daughter, Fatima, and cousin/son-in-law, Ali. He is therefore a descendant of the early Fatimid Caliphs of Cairo.

Location

Azhar Park is situated at 123 Park Avenue, Downtown City, State 12345. It's easily accessible by public transport, with the nearest bus stop just 5 minutes away. Parking is available on-site for visitors.

Facilities

  • Walking Trails: Over 5 miles of paved paths for jogging and cycling.
  • Picnic Areas: Shaded spots with tables and grills.
  • Playground: Safe equipment for children under 12.
  • Sports Fields: Basketball courts and open fields for soccer.
  • Restrooms: Clean facilities available near the main entrance.
  • Visitor Center: Information desk with maps and park history.
